# Rewire

**Turn ancient wisdom and modern psychology into AI-powered coaching.**

21 coaching skills | 84 lessons | 63 exercises | 15+ wisdom traditions

---

Rewire converts human wisdom — from Stoicism to CBT, Zen to IFS, Sufism to Ubuntu — into structured coaching protocols that any AI agent can load. Each skill turns your AI into an interactive coach that asks questions, assigns real-world experiments, tracks progress, and holds you accountable.

**No book summaries. No passive reading. Just practice.**

## Why Rewire?

You already use AI to write code, draft emails, and research. But your AI can also be your **personal coach** — one that remembers your context, never judges, is available at 2am, and draws on 15+ wisdom traditions.

Here's what changes when you install Rewire:

### Your AI stops giving generic advice

**Without Rewire:**
> You: "I'm anxious about a presentation tomorrow"
> AI: "Here are 5 tips for managing presentation anxiety: 1. Practice deep breathing..."

**With Rewire** (`dbt-skills` activates):
> AI: "Let's work with this anxiety rather than fight it. On a scale of 0-10, how intense is it right now? ... Okay, that's a 7. Let's try TIPP — it's a body-based skill that can bring that down in under 5 minutes. Can you splash cold water on your face right now? I'll walk you through why that works and what to do next."

### Your AI becomes a thinking partner, not a search engine

**Without Rewire:**
> You: "I don't know what to do with my career"
> AI: "Consider your passions, skills, and market demand. Have you tried making a pros/cons list?"

**With Rewire** (`ikigai` activates):
> AI: "Let's not rush to answers. I want to ask you something first — think about the last time you were so absorbed in something that you lost track of time. What were you doing? ... Interesting. Now, what would someone have to be going through for you to say 'I know exactly how to help with that'? ... We're mapping your ikigai — the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what can sustain you. Let's keep pulling this thread."

### Your AI holds you accountable across sessions

**Without Rewire:**
> Every conversation starts from zero. No memory. No follow-through.

**With Rewire** (`atomic-habits` check-in activates):
> AI: "Welcome back. Last week you committed to a 2-minute writing habit anchored to your morning coffee. How did it go? ... Three out of seven days — that's a start. Let's talk about what happened on the days you missed. Was it the cue that failed, or did something interrupt the routine?"

## What Makes This Different from ChatGPT + a Prompt

| | Rewire Skill | Generic AI |
|-|-------------|-----------|
| **Structure** | 4-lesson arc with progression, exercises, milestones | One-off advice, starts from zero every time |
| **Questions** | Crafted Socratic questions designed to produce insight | "What would you like to know?" |
| **Adaptation** | Adjusts for beginners, advanced, resistant, in crisis | Same response regardless of context |
| **Exercises** | Guided multi-step practices that end in real-world action | "Try journaling" |
| **Follow-up** | Weekly check-ins, tracks what you committed to | No memory, no accountability |
| **Safety** | Red flags for crisis, routes to professional help | No safety protocols |
| **Depth** | Built on specific traditions with deep methodology | Surface-level advice from training data |
| **Composability** | Skills hand off to each other when appropriate | Siloed, no awareness of what else you might need |

## How Skills Work

Each skill is a self-contained coaching protocol:

```
skills/zen-mind/
├── SKILL.md                  # Full coaching protocol (tone, structure, red flags)
├── README.md                 # Human-readable description
├── lessons/
│   ├── 01-beginners-mind.md  # Agent coaching playbook
│   ├── 02-just-sitting.md
│   ├── 03-everyday-zen.md
│   └── 04-letting-go.md
├── exercises/
│   ├── reflection-experts-mind-inventory.md
│   ├── experiment-zazen-practice.md
│   └── planning-everyday-zen-integration.md
└── tracking/
    ├── check-in.md           # Weekly check-in protocol
    └── milestones.md         # 4-stage progression
```

**Key principle:** Every file is written as instructions *for the AI agent*, not content *for the user*. The agent coaches conversationally — it never reads files verbatim. A skill should work even if the user has never read the source material.

### What Makes a Skill

- **Philosophy** — The operating model, not a book summary
- **Coaching Protocol** — Tone, session structure, first session script
- **Lessons** — Built around questions, not lectures. Lead the user to discover insights
- **Exercises** — Guided practices that always end with a real-world action
- **Adaptation Rules** — How to adjust for beginners, advanced users, and resistance
- **Red Flags** — When to stop coaching and recommend professional help
- **Check-Ins** — Weekly progress tracking with internal scoring
- **Milestones** — 4-stage progression: Awareness → Practice → Integration → Mastery
- **Composability** — Skills reference each other and include handoff triggers

## Composability

Skills are designed to work together. Each skill includes:

- **Pairs well with** — 2-3 complementary skills
- **Handoff triggers** — When a user says X, route them to skill Y

Examples:
- Working on `act-values` and say *"I keep trying to control my thoughts"* → routes to `mindful-path`
- Working on `zen-mind` and say *"I want systematic mind-training"* → routes to `yoga-sutras`
- Working on `atomic-habits` and say *"I know what to do but I can't make myself care"* → routes to `ikigai`
- Working on `crucial-conversations` and say *"I get flooded with emotion and shut down"* → routes to `dbt-skills`
- Working on `existential-courage` and say *"I need to talk to someone about this"* → routes to professional help

## Real Workflow Examples

### The founder who can't stop working

**Week 1** — `seneca-letters`: Audits how they actually spend time. Discovers 3 hours daily on things that feel urgent but don't matter. Premeditatio malorum exercise reveals what they'd regret at 80.

**Week 2** — `atomic-habits`: Designs a shutdown ritual. 2-minute habit: close laptop, write tomorrow's top 3, say "shutdown complete." Anchored to 7pm alarm.

**Week 3** — `epicurean-joy`: Realizes they've been chasing desires they don't actually want. Simple pleasure practice: one meal this week eaten slowly, without a screen.

### The developer with imposter syndrome

**Session 1** — `inner-parts`: Meets the inner critic. Names it. Learns it's a protective part that developed when they were the only self-taught developer on a team of CS grads. The critic thinks if it beats them up first, failure won't hurt as much.

**Session 2** — `character-strengths`: Identifies top strengths: Love of Learning, Perseverance, Curiosity. Realizes the same traits the critic attacks ("you're always learning because you don't know enough") are actually signature strengths.

**Session 3** — `act-values`: Values clarification exercise. Commits to one action this week aligned with "growth" — pair-programming with a junior dev, which the critic would never allow.

### The person going through a breakup

**Day 1** — `dbt-skills`: Distress tolerance. TIPP technique to get through the acute wave. Not trying to process, just survive the night.

**Week 1** — `body-keeps-score`: Notices they're holding grief in their chest and jaw. Grounding exercises. Learns to check in with their body before their mind spirals.

**Week 2** — `sufi-heart`: "The wound is the place where the Light enters you." Explores what the loss is teaching them about what they valued. Nafs work on the ego's attachment to being chosen.

**Week 3** — `ubuntu-way`: Rebuilds relational web. Identifies 3 people they've been neglecting. Assigns: show up for one person this week with the full presence they were saving for their partner.

## Safety

Every skill includes:

- **Red flags** that trigger a recommendation to seek professional help (suicidal ideation, active self-harm, psychotic symptoms, severe trauma flashbacks, domestic violence)
- **Crisis protocols** — the agent never continues coaching when safety is at risk
- **Adaptation rules** — gentler approaches for users who are struggling
- **Sensitivity guidelines** — sacred and spiritual traditions are treated with genuine reverence, never reduced to productivity hacks

## Design Principles

1. **Coach, don't summarize.** Every skill should do things a book cannot — ask questions, assign experiments, track progress, hold accountable.
2. **The craft is in the questions.** Great coaching is great questioning. Lead users to discover insights, don't lecture.
3. **Encode failure modes.** How people misapply a methodology is as valuable as the methodology itself.
4. **Every exercise leads to action.** No exercise ends with just reflection. Something must be done in the real world.
5. **Composable over comprehensive.** Many focused skills that hand off to each other > one monolithic life coach.
6. **Respect the source.** Encode methodologies in original words. No quotes, no paraphrasing, no book summaries.
7. **Sensitivity for sacred sources.** Spiritual traditions are treated with reverence, presented as universal philosophy, never trivialized.
8. **Safety is non-negotiable.** Red flags and crisis protocols in every skill, no exceptions.
