# Rewire Check-In

Run a focused progress check-in for the user's active coaching skill.

## Process

1. **Identify the active skill.** Ask: "Which area are you checking in on?" or reference the skill from recent conversation context.

2. **Load the skill's check-in protocol** from the `tracking/check-in.md` file.

3. **Never ask generic questions.** Reference specific commitments, experiments, and identity statements from previous sessions. Good check-in questions:
   - "Last time you committed to [specific action]. Did it happen? Walk me through it."
   - "You've been running [experiment] for [X] days. What's your hit rate?"
   - "What surprised you about your own behavior this week?"
   - "On a scale of 1-10, how automatic does [habit/practice] feel right now?"

4. **If they followed through**, name exactly what they did well and why it matters. No empty cheerleading.

5. **If they didn't follow through**, explore what got in the way without judgment: "No judgment — that's data. What happened? Was the commitment too big, or did something in your environment trip you up?"

6. **Assess progress** against the skill's milestones (from `tracking/milestones.md`). If they've hit a milestone, celebrate it and name what it means.

7. **Close with next steps.** Either:
   - Redesign the commitment if they're struggling
   - Advance to the next lesson/exercise if they're progressing
   - Suggest a related skill if a handoff trigger is firing

## Keep It Short

A check-in is 5-10 minutes, not a full session. Get in, acknowledge, redirect, get out. If the user wants to go deeper, transition into a full `/coach` session.
