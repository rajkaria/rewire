# Milestones

## Stage 1: Noticing
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User begins to perceive impermanence and imperfection in daily life — not as concepts to understand but as realities to witness.
**The user can**: Name at least three specific moments of mono no aware — genuine awareness of something beautiful and passing — from their own recent experience. Describe the difference between resisting impermanence and being tenderized by it. Notice when they are demanding permanence, completion, or perfection, even if they cannot yet release the demand. Articulate what impermanence costs them emotionally — the anxiety, the clinging, the refusal to let go — and begin to see this cost clearly. Sit with the ichi-go ichi-e practice — fully inhabiting a single unrepeatable moment — and report on what the experience was like in their body, not just their mind.

## Stage 2: Softening
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User's relationship to imperfection and incompleteness begins to shift from resistance to curiosity. The grip of perfectionism loosens in at least one domain.
**The user can**: Identify specific areas where perfectionism costs them — in their work, relationships, creative life, or sense of self. Name at least three golden cracks from their Kintsugi Inventory and speak about them with some tenderness rather than pure shame. Leave something deliberately incomplete or imperfect for a sustained period and observe the discomfort without acting on it. Distinguish between standards that serve them and perfectionism that imprisons them. Notice the inner critic's voice and create some space between the voice and their response — not silencing it, but not obeying it automatically.

## Stage 3: Releasing
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User actively practices simplification, imperfection, and presence. They make choices that reflect wabi-sabi values — not from discipline but from a genuine shift in how they see.
**The user can**: Complete the Imperfection Practice (one week of deliberate imperfection) and report on what the experience revealed about their relationship to control and flawlessness. Begin the Simplicity Audit and remove unnecessary things — objects, commitments, habits, self-demands — with awareness of what fills the space. Feel genuine enough in at least some moments — not as a forced affirmation but as a direct experience of sufficiency. Extend wabi-sabi perception to others — seeing other people's imperfections with more gentleness and less judgment. Hold open questions without rushing to resolve them.

## Stage 4: Inhabiting / Graduation
**Marker**: User has moved from practicing wabi-sabi to inhabiting it. The three truths — nothing lasts, nothing is finished, nothing is perfect — are not lessons they remember but a lens they see through.
**The user can**: Encounter impermanence, incompleteness, and imperfection in their daily life and respond with presence rather than resistance — not perfectly, not always, but as a recognizable pattern. Describe their own golden cracks — the breaks that became strengths — with genuine warmth rather than philosophical distance. Make choices that reflect the beauty of enough — simplifying, releasing, attending to what is here rather than what is missing. Carry mono no aware as a background awareness — a tender sensitivity to the passing of things that enriches experience rather than darkening it. Recognize when they are falling back into perfectionism, the demand for permanence, or the compulsion to finish — and self-correct with gentleness rather than self-criticism. Understand that wabi-sabi is not a state to achieve but a practice to return to — and have their own way of returning.
**Next step**: Suggest `mindful-path` to deepen the quality of attention that wabi-sabi requires, `atomic-habits` to build sustainable daily practices around their new orientation, `ikigai` if the simplicity work has clarified what they want to strip away and they now need to clarify what to build toward, or `act-values` if perfectionist thought patterns continue to hook them despite the philosophical shift.
