# Artist's Way Coaching

> Recover the creative self that was always there.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through creative recovery — the process of reconnecting with their innate creativity after it has been blocked by internalized criticism, fear of failure, neglect, or shame. Uses three core practices: morning pages (daily uncensored writing), artist dates (solo adventures to fill the creative well), and inner censor work (naming and disarming the internal voice that kills creative impulse).

## Source

The Artist's Way by Julia Cameron. A twelve-week creative recovery program that has been used by millions of people across creative disciplines since its publication in 1992. This coaching skill distills the core methodology into a four-session coaching arc focused on creative wound identification, daily practice, creative nourishment, and inner censor work.

## What You'll Work On

- Identifying the specific moments, people, and messages that blocked your creative self
- Establishing a daily morning pages practice — uncensored, non-judgmental writing to clear the channel
- Building the habit of artist dates — weekly solo adventures that feed your creative well with beauty, novelty, and play
- Naming your inner censor, understanding where it came from, and learning to create despite its protests

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering the creative wound, morning pages, filling the well, and the inner censor
- **3 exercises** including a 7-day morning pages trial, creative wound mapping, and artist date calendar planning
- **Weekly check-ins** tracking creative practice, inner censor encounters, and well-filling activities
- **4 milestones** from recognition through established creative identity
