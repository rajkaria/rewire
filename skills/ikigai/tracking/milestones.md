# Milestones

## Stage 1: Awareness
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User reconnects with genuine sources of aliveness and begins distinguishing between authentic interests and inherited expectations.
**The user can**: Name at least five specific activities, topics, or moments that bring genuine energy. Distinguish between what they love and what they think they should love. Identify at least three energizing strengths (skills that give them energy, not just competence). Recognize the difference between passion and obligation in their daily life.

## Stage 2: Exploration
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User actively explores all four circles and begins identifying overlaps and gaps. They have moved beyond reflection into real-world inquiry.
**The user can**: Articulate their top entries for all four ikigai circles in their own words. Identify at least one specific need in the world that calls to them personally. See at least one area of overlap between two or more circles. Have had at least one conversation with someone in a field or role related to their emerging direction. Complete the Life Audit exercise and score their current alignment honestly.

## Stage 3: Alignment
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User has a working ikigai hypothesis and has tested it through at least one real-world experiment. They are beginning to make life decisions informed by their four-circle analysis.
**The user can**: State their ikigai hypothesis in one to two specific sentences. Design and execute purpose prototypes without needing step-by-step coaching. Evaluate experiments honestly — recognizing both confirmation and disconfirmation of their hypothesis. Identify where reward and sustainability fit into their direction. Begin making small but real adjustments to their time, energy, or career trajectory based on alignment.

## Stage 4: Integration / Graduation
**Marker**: User has a lived sense of their ikigai direction and is actively building their life around it. They hold their purpose lightly, knowing it will evolve.
**The user can**: Describe their ikigai — the intersection of love, skill, need, and reward — in their own words with concrete specificity. Point to real-world evidence from their experiments that supports this direction. Make daily and weekly choices that increase alignment across all four circles. Recognize when they drift out of alignment and self-correct without coaching. Understand that ikigai is a living direction, not a fixed destination, and have a practice for ongoing recalibration.
**Next step**: Suggest `deep-work` to build sustained focus on their ikigai direction, `karma-yoga` if service is central and they need to learn detached giving, `finding-meaning` if existential questions persist beneath the directional clarity, or `atomic-habits` to build daily systems that support their chosen path.
