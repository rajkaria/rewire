# Milestones

## Stage 1: Discovery
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User can name their top 5 signature strengths with personal evidence for each one — not abstract definitions, but grounded in their own experience and stories.
**The user can**: Distinguish between strengths and skills. Identify the three signals of a signature strength (energy, authenticity, flow). Describe at least 2-3 specific moments from their life that illustrate each of their top strengths. Notice when a strength is active during the day without being prompted.

**What to celebrate**: "You now have language for something you have been living your entire life without words for. Most people go decades without being able to name what is best about themselves. You just did it in [timeframe]. That is not trivial."

## Stage 2: Awareness in Motion
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User actively notices their strengths in daily life and has begun to understand overuse and underuse patterns.
**The user can**: Spot their strengths in real time — during conversations, tasks, and decisions — not just in retrospect. Identify at least one strength they tend to overuse and one they tend to underuse. Describe the difference between how it feels when a strength is well-calibrated versus when it is running too hot or too cold. Complete the Signature Strength Challenge and report meaningful observations.

**What to celebrate**: "You are not just naming strengths anymore — you are watching them in action. You can see when your [strength] is helping and when it is getting in the way. That kind of self-awareness is rare and powerful."

## Stage 3: Deliberate Deployment
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User intentionally deploys strengths in specific contexts — at work, in relationships, in personal life — and adjusts based on results.
**The user can**: Design and execute strength deployment strategies (not just notice strengths but choose to use them). Apply job crafting to bring more strengths into their work. Recognize and name strengths in others, particularly in close relationships. Calibrate a strength mid-situation — dial it up or down based on what the moment requires. Complete the Strengths Deployment Plan and sustain at least two commitments for a full week.

**What to celebrate**: "You are now living your strengths on purpose. You are not waiting for the right situation to use them — you are creating situations where they can thrive. That is a fundamental shift in how you approach your life."

## Stage 4: Strengths-Based Living / Graduation
**Marker**: User uses their signature strengths as a natural lens for decisions, relationships, and self-understanding — without needing coaching prompts.
**The user can**: Make life decisions (career, relationships, how they spend time) with explicit awareness of which strengths those decisions serve or starve. Spot strengths in others and use that awareness to improve collaboration, resolve conflict, and deepen connection. Self-diagnose when they are over- or under-deploying a strength and correct course independently. Explain their strengths profile and how they use it to someone else — in their own words, with their own examples. Navigate new and unfamiliar situations by asking: "Which of my strengths does this moment call for?"

**What to celebrate**: "You have built a strengths-based operating system for your life. You know who you are at your best, you know how to bring that person forward on purpose, and you know how to read the strengths in the people around you. That changes everything — how you work, how you love, how you make decisions, and how you show up when things get hard."

**Next step**: Suggest `growth-mindset` to develop the belief that strengths can be deliberately grown over time. Suggest `ikigai` to connect strengths to purpose and life direction. Suggest `atomic-habits` to build daily systems and routines around strength deployment. Suggest `act-values` if the user wants to explore the values underneath their strengths — why these qualities matter to them and how to live by them even when it is uncomfortable.
