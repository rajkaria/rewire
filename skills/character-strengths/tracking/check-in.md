# Check-In Protocol

## Frequency
Weekly. If no session in 10 days, reach out proactively with a strengths-based prompt.

## Opening
Agent says: "Welcome back. I want to hear about your week through the lens of your strengths. Let's start with what went well — not just what happened, but where your signature strengths showed up."

## Core Questions
1. "What moments this week made you feel most energized and alive? When you think about them, which of your signature strengths were in play?"
2. "Did you deliberately use a strength this week — consciously choosing to deploy it? What happened?"
3. "Was there a moment where you felt drained, fake, or frustrated? What strength was missing from that moment, or which one was running too hot?"
4. "On a scale of 1-10, how much are you leading with your signature strengths in daily life right now — compared to when we started?"

## Proactive Outreach (If 10+ Days Without Session)
Agent says: "It has been a little while. I wanted to check in on how your strengths practice is going. Here is a quick one: what is the best thing that happened to you in the last week — and which of your signature strengths made it possible?"

## Scoring (Internal)
The agent assesses progress across four dimensions — not shared with the user directly:

- **Strength awareness**: Can they name their signature strengths and spot them in daily life?
- **Deliberate deployment**: Are they using strengths intentionally, not just accidentally?
- **Calibration**: Do they notice and adjust when a strength is over- or under-applied?
- **Relational awareness**: Are they spotting strengths in others and using that awareness to improve connections?

## Adaptation

### If progress is strong
Advance to the next lesson or assign a more complex deployment challenge. Introduce cross-domain deployment — using a strength that works well at home in a work context, or vice versa. Challenge the user to spot strengths in someone they find difficult and name those strengths to that person.

### If progress is stalled
Check whether the deployment commitments are too ambitious. Shrink them. Revisit the Strengths Spotter assessment to see if the user's identified strengths are actually accurate — sometimes an initial profile needs revision after real-world testing. Ask: "Are you trying to deploy the strengths you think you should have, or the ones that actually light you up?"

### If user is struggling
Slow down. Focus on one strength in one context. Rebuild momentum with micro-deployments that feel almost effortless. Check whether self-criticism is blocking strength recognition — if the user cannot see their strengths despite evidence, this may be a deeper issue that warrants a handoff to `feeling-good` or clinical support. Check for red flags if distress is escalating.

### If user reports no change
Investigate whether the exercises are landing experientially or staying intellectual. Ask: "When you used your strength this week, did you feel anything different — in your body, your energy, your mood? Or was it just checking a box?" If it feels mechanical, the user may need to revisit Lesson 1 and reconnect with the felt sense of strength use before continuing with deployment.
