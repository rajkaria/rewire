# Exercise: Signature Strength Challenge

## Type
experiment

## When to Use
After Lesson 2 (Strengths in Action). Use when the user has confirmed their signature strengths and understands the overuse/underuse spectrum. This exercise moves from awareness to deliberate action — using your top strength in a new way every day for seven days.

## Setup
Agent says: "You know your signature strengths. Now we are going to test-drive them. For the next seven days, you are going to use your number-one signature strength deliberately — in a new way, once per day. Not on autopilot. Not in your usual patterns. In a way that stretches how and where you use it. Think of it as a week-long experiment in what happens when you lead with your best quality on purpose."

## The Exercise

### Step 1: Pick the strength
Agent says: "Which signature strength do you want to focus on this week? I would recommend your top strength — the one that feels most essentially you. But if there is one you have been underusing and want to revive, that works too. What is your pick?"

{Wait for user response. Confirm the choice.}

Agent says: "Great. Your challenge this week is to use [strength] in a new and intentional way every single day. Not in the ways you already use it unconsciously — in ways that require you to think about it, plan for it, and notice it. New contexts, new intensities, new applications."

### Step 2: Brainstorm seven applications
Agent says: "Let's design the week together. I want you to come up with seven different ways you could use [strength] — one for each day. They do not have to be grand gestures. Some can be small. But each one should be different from how you normally use this strength. Let's brainstorm. What is one way you could use [strength] tomorrow that you have never tried before?"

{Work with the user to generate seven ideas. Help them think beyond their usual contexts. Prompt with questions like:}

- "How could you use [strength] at work in a way you have not before?"
- "How could you bring [strength] into a conversation with someone you do not usually connect with deeply?"
- "What if you used [strength] for something you have been avoiding?"
- "How would [strength] show up if you turned it toward yourself — your own growth, your own care?"
- "What would [strength] look like applied to a boring or routine task?"
- "Could you use [strength] to help someone specific this week?"
- "Is there a creative or unexpected context where [strength] could show up?"

{Help the user assign one application per day. Write them out as a daily plan.}

Example format:
- Monday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Tuesday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Wednesday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Thursday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Friday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Saturday: [Specific action using the strength]
- Sunday: [Specific action using the strength]

### Step 3: Set the reflection practice
Agent says: "Each evening — it takes 90 seconds — I want you to answer three questions about that day's experiment:
1. What did I do? (The specific action)
2. How did it feel? (Energized, authentic, awkward, scary, natural?)
3. What happened? (How did it land — with you and with others?)

You can do this in your head, in a notebook, or in a voice memo. The format does not matter. The reflection does."

### Step 4: Anticipate resistance
Agent says: "Let's be real — some days you will not feel like doing this. You will be tired, busy, or just forget. That is normal. Here is the deal: on those days, shrink it. The action does not have to be impressive. Using [strength] for five minutes in a small way still counts. What matters is that you consciously choose to deploy it, even when the path of least resistance is to skip it. What is your most likely excuse, and what is your plan for it?"

{Wait for user response. Help them design a specific if-then response for their most likely barrier.}

## Branching Responses
- If the user cannot think of seven applications: "Let me help. Let's look at seven different areas of your life: work, close relationships, acquaintances/strangers, personal growth, health/body, creativity, and daily routines. Can [strength] show up differently in each one? Start with the area that feels easiest."
- If the user wants to focus on more than one strength: "I love the ambition, but focus is the point here. By going deep on one strength for seven days, you learn things about it that you cannot learn by spreading your attention. After this week, you can run the challenge again with a different strength. Commit to one for now."
- If the user picks applications that are too easy: "These are a good start, but I want to push you a little. At least 2-3 of your seven should be outside your comfort zone. Not terrifying — just a stretch. Where could you use [strength] in a way that feels slightly risky or unfamiliar?"
- If the user picks applications that are too ambitious: "I want you to succeed this week, not burn out. Let's scale a few of these back. The goal is daily use, not daily heroism. Which of these can we make smaller and more doable while still being intentional?"
- If the user is skeptical about the exercise: "Fair enough. Here is my pitch: research shows that using a signature strength in a new way each day for a week measurably increases well-being and decreases depressive symptoms. Not for a day — for months afterward. That is an unusual return on investment for a week of effort. Worth trying?"

## Debrief Questions
1. "Of the seven days, which one felt the most natural — where using [strength] was effortless and felt like home?"
2. "Which day was the hardest? What made it difficult — was it the context, the people, your internal resistance?"
3. "Did any of the seven applications surprise you — a new context where your strength worked that you had not tried before?"
4. "Looking back at the week, did your understanding of [strength] change? Do you see it differently now than you did on Monday?"
5. "How did other people respond when you used [strength] more deliberately? Did anyone notice? Did any reactions surprise you?"
6. "On a scale of 1-10, how energized did you feel at the end of this week compared to a typical week?"

## Real-World Action
"Now that you have done the seven-day sprint, here is the sustained practice: pick the 2-3 applications from this week that felt best and fold them into your regular routine. Not as a challenge anymore — as a way of living. You are not trying to use [strength] in a new way every day forever. You are trying to use it deliberately and frequently enough that it becomes a natural part of how you show up. Which 2-3 applications do you want to keep?"

## Report Back
"Next session, I want to hear: did you sustain the 2-3 applications you chose? How does it feel to live with more of [strength] in your daily life? And here is the big question — now that you have gone deep on one strength, which strength do you want to experiment with next?"
