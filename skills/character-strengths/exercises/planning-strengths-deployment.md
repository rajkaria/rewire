# Exercise: Strengths Deployment Plan

## Type
planning

## When to Use
After Lesson 3 (Strengths at Work). Use when the user has a solid understanding of their signature strengths, knows their overuse/underuse patterns, and is ready to design specific situations where they will deploy underused or misaligned strengths. This exercise is about strategic, intentional change — not just awareness.

## Setup
Agent says: "You have identified your strengths. You have practiced using your top strength in new ways. Now we are going to do something more targeted: we are going to find the specific areas of your life where a strength is underused or misapplied, and design a deployment plan to change that. This is where strengths work moves from interesting to transformative — when you start deliberately restructuring how you live around who you actually are."

## The Exercise

### Step 1: Map the current state
Agent says: "I want you to think about four domains of your life: work, relationships, personal growth, and health/self-care. For each one, I want you to answer two questions: (1) which of your signature strengths are you using regularly in this domain? (2) which of your signature strengths are starving — they barely get to show up?"

{Walk through each domain one at a time.}

**Work:**
Agent says: "Starting with work. Which of your top strengths are active in your daily work? And which ones rarely get to show up?"

{Wait for response. Note the active and starving strengths.}

**Relationships:**
Agent says: "Now relationships — partner, family, close friends. Which strengths do you bring to your closest connections? And which strengths are hidden or suppressed?"

{Wait for response}

**Personal Growth:**
Agent says: "Personal growth — hobbies, learning, creative pursuits, anything you do for your own development. Which strengths fuel this area? Which are absent?"

{Wait for response}

**Health and Self-Care:**
Agent says: "Finally, health and self-care — exercise, rest, nutrition, mental health practices. How do your strengths show up here? Any that are completely missing?"

{Wait for response}

### Step 2: Identify the biggest gaps
Agent says: "Looking across all four domains, where are the biggest mismatches? I am looking for two things: (1) a signature strength that is barely showing up anywhere — it is genuinely starving, and (2) a domain where you feel the most drain or disconnection, which might be because the strengths you need there are not being deployed."

{Help the user identify 1-2 critical gaps.}

Agent says: "These gaps are not random. When a signature strength is starving, you feel it — as boredom, frustration, inauthenticity, or restlessness. The gap you are feeling in [domain] might be directly connected to the fact that [strength] has no room to operate there. Does that resonate?"

### Step 3: Design deployment strategies
Agent says: "Now let's fix it. For each gap, we are going to design a specific deployment strategy. Not a vague intention — a concrete plan with a context, a trigger, and an action."

{For each identified gap, walk through this template:}

**Gap: [Strength] is underused in [Domain]**

Agent says: "Let's start with [strength] in [domain]. What would it actually look like to bring more [strength] into this part of your life? I want a specific scenario. When would you use it? Where? With whom? What would you do differently than you do now?"

{Wait for response}

Agent says: "Good. Now let's make it concrete. Fill in this sentence: 'When [specific trigger or situation], I will use my [strength] by [specific action].' For example: 'When I am in my weekly team meeting and someone proposes an idea, I will use my curiosity by asking two genuine questions before evaluating.' What is yours?"

{Wait for response. Refine the statement until it is specific and actionable.}

Agent says: "Now the hard part: what will get in the way? What thought, feeling, or habit is most likely to prevent you from doing this? And what is your plan for that barrier?"

{Wait for response. Help them anticipate and plan for obstacles.}

### Step 4: Address overuse corrections
Agent says: "We have been talking about deploying underused strengths. Let's also look at strengths you might be overdeploying. Is there a strength that is causing friction in any of these domains — one that is running too hot and needs to be dialed back in specific situations?"

{Wait for response}

If the user identifies an overuse pattern:
Agent says: "Let's design a correction strategy. Same format: 'When [trigger situation], instead of [overuse behavior], I will [alternative behavior that uses a different strength or the same strength at lower intensity].' What would that look like?"

{Wait for response}

### Step 5: Build the weekly practice
Agent says: "Let's turn all of this into a simple weekly plan. You should not try to change everything at once. Pick the two most important deployment strategies — one underused strength to bring online and one overused strength to calibrate — and commit to practicing them this week."

Agent says: "Write them down in this format:
1. Deploy [underused strength] in [domain]: When [trigger], I will [action].
2. Calibrate [overused strength] in [domain]: When [trigger], instead of [overuse], I will [alternative].

These are your two strength commitments for this week. Can you rate your confidence in following through on each one, 1-10?"

{If confidence is below 7 on either, help them shrink the commitment until confidence rises.}

## Branching Responses
- If the user cannot identify underused strengths: "Let me try a different approach. Think about the last time you felt really alive — completely in your element. Now compare that to your average Tuesday. What strength was present in the first moment and absent in the second? That is your underused strength."
- If the user wants to deploy all their strengths everywhere: "That is a good long-term vision. But trying to change everything at once usually means changing nothing. Two commitments for this week. You can add more next week. What are the two that would make the biggest difference right now?"
- If the user feels their life has no room for underused strengths: "That feeling is itself useful data. It might mean your life has been designed around other people's expectations rather than your own strengths. Even small insertions matter — five minutes of [strength] per day is better than zero. Where is there any room at all?"
- If the user identifies overuse but resists changing it: "Overuse often feels like 'just being myself,' which makes it hard to calibrate. But here is the question: is this pattern serving you, or is it causing problems? If it is causing friction with people you care about, calibration is not compromising your identity — it is using your strength more wisely."
- If the user is excited and wants to do more: "I love the energy. Channel it into depth, not breadth. Master these two deployments this week. Make them automatic. Then we add more. Speed without consistency is just enthusiasm that fades."

## Debrief Questions
1. "Did you follow through on your two commitments this week? What happened?"
2. "When you deployed [underused strength], how did it feel? Awkward? Energizing? Both?"
3. "When you calibrated [overused strength], what did you notice? Was it harder or easier than expected?"
4. "Did anyone else notice a change in how you showed up? What feedback did you get — explicit or implicit?"
5. "Looking at your four-domain map now, has anything shifted? Any domain feel more aligned than it did a week ago?"

## Real-World Action
"Keep your two deployment strategies active for another week. If they are working, they should start feeling more natural by the end of week two. Once they feel semi-automatic, we add a third. The goal is to gradually reshape how you live so that your signature strengths have room to breathe in every domain — not just the ones that happened to give them space already."

## Report Back
"Next session, bring your observations: which deployment stuck, which one was harder than expected, and what ripple effects did you notice? Also tell me whether your four-domain map has shifted at all. We are building toward a life where your strengths are not occasional guests — they are residents."
