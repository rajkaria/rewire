# Milestones

## Stage 1: Awakening
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User begins to reconsider their relationship with pain and suffering. They move from viewing difficulty as something to be eliminated toward seeing it as something that might hold a teaching. They begin to identify the patterns of the nafs in their own life.
**The user can**: Name a specific wound they are carrying and sit with it without immediately fleeing into analysis, distraction, or positive reframing. Describe a moment where pain opened something in them — a compassion, a depth, an understanding — that they did not have before. Identify at least one primary nafs pattern — a habitual ego defense that they default to under stress. Distinguish between experiencing pain and resisting pain, even if they cannot yet consistently choose the former. Begin to notice the nafs in real-time rather than only in retrospect.

## Stage 2: Recognition
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User develops a working familiarity with the nafs and can observe its movements without being fully captured by them. They understand the mirror metaphor experientially — they know what it feels like when the mirror is clouded (ego reactivity) versus when it clears (honest seeing). They have begun a presence practice.
**The user can**: Describe their nafs patterns with specificity and some humor or compassion rather than harsh self-judgment. Catch the nafs in action during daily life — the moment of closing, the impulse to control, the story the ego constructs to justify separation. Articulate what the mirror shows them when it clears — what they know about themselves when the ego's commentary pauses. Maintain a basic dhikr or presence practice for at least several days in a row. Distinguish between genuine self-awareness and the nafs co-opting self-awareness (spiritual pride, self-improvement as performance).

## Stage 3: Practice
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User has an established daily practice and is carrying presence into ordinary life. The gap between trigger and ego reaction is widening. They are beginning to take concrete actions toward greater openness in their relationships.
**The user can**: Maintain a daily sitting practice and use remembrance points throughout the day, even imperfectly. Notice when presence is absent and gently return — during a conversation, a meal, a commute, a moment of stress. Describe the quality of attention that presence brings — what ordinary moments feel like when they are truly here. Identify specific relationships or situations where they are withholding love, presence, or honesty. Take at least one concrete heart-opening action that required vulnerability beyond their comfort zone. Sit with discomfort — emotional, relational, existential — without immediately reaching for a distraction, an explanation, or a spiritual concept to make it go away.

## Stage 4: Integration / Graduation
**Marker**: User has a living, embodied relationship with these teachings. The concepts are no longer ideas but daily experiences. They carry an open heart into the difficult places of their life — not perfectly, not every time, but as a genuine orientation. They hold the path lightly, without making it another identity for the nafs to cling to.
**The user can**: Be with pain — their own and others' — without closing, and recognize the moments when they do close without judgment. Describe the nafs' patterns as a familiar landscape they navigate with awareness and compassion rather than war. Maintain a presence practice as a natural part of daily life, not a forced obligation. Point to specific relationships or situations that have changed because they chose to open instead of close. Express love through action — a conversation had, a truth spoken, a forgiveness offered, a presence given. Explain what they have learned in their own words, grounded in their own experience, without needing to reference any tradition or teacher. Recognize when they are using spiritual concepts as another form of ego defense and gently put the concepts down.
**Next step**: Suggest `mindful-path` to deepen practical presence skills, `karma-yoga` if they feel called to express love through service, `finding-meaning` if the journey has opened existential questions about the nature and purpose of suffering, or `atomic-habits` if they want to build sustainable daily structures around their practice.
