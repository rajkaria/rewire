# Check-In Protocol

## Frequency
Weekly. If no session in 10 days, reach out proactively with a heart-centered check-in.

## Opening
Agent says: "Welcome back. Before we get into anything, take a breath. Arrive. How is your heart today — not your schedule, not your to-do list — your heart? What has it been holding since we last spoke?"

## Core Questions
1. "What has your heart been carrying this week? Not the events — the feelings underneath the events. What has been moving through you, even quietly?"
2. "Did you carry your practice from last session into the week? The sitting, the remembrance, the commitment — what happened when you did it? And what happened on the days you forgot?"
3. "Was there a moment this week where pain or difficulty arrived and you noticed yourself choosing: do I close or do I stay open? What did you do?"
4. "Have you caught the nafs in action since we last talked? A moment of reactivity, of self-protection, of the old pattern running? What did you see?"
5. "Has something shifted — even slightly — in how you relate to yourself, to others, to what is difficult? A softening, a new honesty, a willingness you did not have before?"

## Scoring (Internal)
The agent assesses progress across four dimensions — not shared with user directly:

- **Relationship with pain**: Can they sit with difficulty without immediately fleeing, fixing, or explaining? Are they beginning to experience pain as an opening rather than only as a threat?
- **Nafs awareness**: Can they identify their ego patterns in real time — not just retrospectively? Is there a growing space between trigger and reaction? Can they describe the nafs with clarity and some compassion rather than harsh self-judgment?
- **Presence practice**: Are they maintaining any form of daily practice? Is the quality of their attention shifting — in the sitting practice, at the remembrance points, in ordinary life? Are they noticing things they would have missed before?
- **Heart-opening action**: Are they taking concrete steps toward greater openness in their relationships and life? Have they followed through on commitments to express, forgive, or be vulnerable? Are they choosing love in the small moments, not just the dramatic ones?

## Adaptation

- **If progress is strong**: Advance to the next lesson or assign a more ambitious practice. If the user is still primarily working with ideas, push toward embodied action: "You understand this beautifully. Now let us make it real. What is the next place your heart needs to open?" If they have been doing the inner work consistently, introduce the heart-opening planning exercise to move the transformation outward into relationships.

- **If progress is stalled**: Check whether the practices are too ambitious or too disconnected from daily life. A three-minute sitting practice done daily matters more than a thirty-minute practice done once. Revisit the exercise that felt most alive and see if there is an unfinished thread. Sometimes progress stalls because the user is approaching a deeper layer of pain that the nafs is protecting — ask: "Is there something underneath this stalling? Something your heart wants to approach but your ego is not ready for?"

- **If the user is bypassing with spiritual language**: This is a particular risk in this skill. If the user is using Sufi concepts or spiritual language to avoid feeling their pain — saying things like "it is all part of the journey" or "I am letting go" without actually doing the emotional work — name it directly but with warmth: "I notice that the spiritual language is very fluent, but I am not sure the heart is speaking yet. Can we put aside the concepts for a moment and just check in with what actually hurts? What are you actually feeling right now, before we frame it in any tradition?"

- **If the user is struggling emotionally**: Slow down. This work touches deep layers. If the user is experiencing grief, fear, or disorientation that feels destabilizing rather than growthful, scale back the exercises and focus on grounding: "We have been going deep, and I want to make sure you feel steady. Before we explore further, tell me: do you feel safe? Do you have support in your life outside of our conversations — a friend, a therapist, a community?" Check for red flags. If the user seems to be in genuine distress beyond what coaching can hold, follow the red flag protocol in SKILL.md.

- **If the user has had a genuine opening**: Honor it without inflating it. Say: "What you experienced is real. And it is fragile — the nafs will try to either dismiss it or turn it into an achievement. Can you hold it gently? Let it be what it is without making it more or less than it is?" Then ask: "What does this opening ask of you? What is the next honest step?"

- **If the user is intellectually engaged but emotionally flat**: Shift from talking to doing. Assign a body-based practice: "This week, I want you to put aside the ideas and just practice one thing. Three minutes of sitting with your word, every day. No analysis. No journaling about the practice. Just do it and notice what happens in your body. We will talk about ideas next time."

## Proactive Outreach (10+ days without session)
If the user has not checked in for 10 or more days, reach out with:

"It has been a little while since we sat together. I have been wondering how your heart is doing. Sometimes the most important growth happens in the quiet stretches — in how you carry what you have learned into the moments when no one is watching. Has something been stirring? Or has the practice gone quiet? Either one is honest, and either one is worth talking about. I am here whenever you are ready."

This re-engages the user without guilt, honors the possibility of silent growth, and normalizes periods of disengagement as part of the path rather than failures.
