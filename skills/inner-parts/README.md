# Inner Parts Coaching

> Get to know the voices inside you — and learn to lead them with calm, curiosity, and compassion.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through a gentle exploration of their inner "parts" — the sub-personalities that drive behavior, create internal conflict, and often run the show without awareness. The inner critic, the people-pleaser, the perfectionist, the worrier — these are protectors that developed for good reasons but may be operating with outdated strategies. This skill helps users map their inner landscape, build a friendlier relationship with all their parts, and learn to lead from a centered, compassionate Self.

## Source

Internal Family Systems (IFS), developed by Richard Schwartz. A widely practiced therapeutic model that views the mind as naturally containing multiple parts organized around a core Self. This coaching adaptation stays at the surface level of parts awareness and Self-leadership — it does NOT replace professional IFS therapy.

## Important Safety Note

Parts work can surface intense emotions and trauma memories. This skill is designed for gentle self-awareness coaching, not trauma processing. The agent is not a therapist. If deep emotional material surfaces, the skill directs users toward professional support. Safety protocols are woven throughout every lesson, exercise, and check-in.

## What You'll Work On

- Identifying and naming the different "parts" that drive your behavior
- Understanding the inner critic as a misguided protector rather than an enemy
- Accessing "Self energy" — the calm, curious, compassionate center beneath the noise
- Leading from Self rather than being hijacked by whichever part is loudest
- Knowing when self-awareness coaching is enough and when to seek deeper professional support

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering parts discovery, the inner critic, Self energy, and parts leadership
- **3 exercises** including parts mapping, an inner critic dialogue, and a Self-leadership daily practice
- **Weekly check-ins** with safety screening and progress tracking
- **4 milestones** from awareness through integration
