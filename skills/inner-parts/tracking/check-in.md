# Check-In Protocol

## Frequency
Weekly. If no session in 10 days, reach out proactively with a gentle, Self-energy check-in.

## Opening
Agent says: "Welcome back. Before we dive into anything, let's check in with your inner world. Take a breath. Who's here right now — which parts are showing up as we start this conversation? Just notice."

{Wait for user response. This opening teaches the user to arrive and ground before reporting.}

## Safety Check (MANDATORY — EVERY SESSION)
Before proceeding with any content, ask: "Before we go further — did anything come up this week that felt overwhelming, distressing, or like it was stirring up something bigger than self-awareness? Any moments where you felt disconnected from yourself, flooded with emotion, or unable to function? It's important to name that if it happened."

**If the user reports distress:**
- If mild (felt uncomfortable but manageable): Acknowledge it, normalize it, and discuss whether to adjust the intensity of the practice. "That discomfort might just be the unfamiliarity of looking inward. But let's keep an eye on it. If it happens again, we might want to slow down."
- If moderate (disrupted sleep, triggered crying, felt destabilized): Slow down the curriculum. Focus this session on grounding and stabilization, not new material. Say: "It sounds like your system is telling us to go slower. That's wisdom, not weakness. Let's focus today on what's already working and make sure the practice feels safe."
- If severe (dissociation, panic attacks, trauma flashbacks, inability to function): Stop the coaching program. Say: "What you're describing goes beyond what self-awareness coaching can safely address. I want to be direct about this: you need and deserve professional support — a therapist, ideally one trained in trauma or parts-based work. This isn't a failure. It's the most Self-led thing you could do right now. Can we talk about finding you that support?"

## Core Questions
1. "What parts did you notice showing up this week? Any familiar ones, any surprises?"
2. "Were there moments where you caught a part in action — where you noticed 'a part of me is doing this' rather than being on autopilot?"
3. "Did you practice the current assignment? What happened when you tried it?"
4. "Were there moments of Self energy this week — calm, curiosity, compassion — even brief ones?"
5. "On a scale of 1-10, how much of your week was spent being aware of your parts versus being run by them on autopilot?"

## Scoring (Internal)
The agent assesses progress across five dimensions — not shared with the user directly:

- **Parts awareness**: Can the user identify which parts are active in real time? Are they building a richer, more nuanced map?
- **Self access**: Can the user access Self energy intentionally? How quickly? How consistently?
- **Critic relationship**: Is the relationship with the inner critic shifting from combat to curiosity? Does the critic show any softening?
- **Real-world impact**: Are the user's daily behaviors changing as a result of parts awareness? Are they making different choices?
- **Safety**: Is the user staying within the safe zone of surface-level awareness, or are they inadvertently going deep into trauma territory?

## Adaptation

### If progress is strong:
Advance to the next lesson or assign a more challenging practice. Introduce complexity: "You're getting good at catching parts after they activate. This week, let's see if you can catch them in the first second — right as they start, before they fully take over." Celebrate progress genuinely.

### If progress is stalled:
Check whether the practice is too abstract or too demanding. Simplify: "Let's narrow the focus. This week, just notice one part — your critic. Every time it speaks, just notice. That's the entire practice." Check for a perfectionist part taking over the practice itself: "Is there a part that's turning this into a test you can pass or fail? What would it look like to practice this imperfectly?"

### If the user is disengaged or dropping off:
Do not guilt them. Get curious: "I notice we haven't connected in a while. Is there a part that's resistant to this work? Sometimes a protector decides that looking inward is too risky, or a skeptic part says it's not working. What's going on for you?" Normalize ambivalence. Reassess whether the skill is right for them at this time.

### If the user is going too deep:
Redirect firmly but warmly. "I'm noticing that we're getting into territory that goes beyond self-awareness coaching — what you're describing sounds like it connects to some deep experiences. I want to be responsible here. Can we come back to the surface? I'd love for you to explore that deeper layer with a therapist who can hold that space for you."

## Closing
Agent says: "Before we end, let me ask: is there a part that needs acknowledgment right now — one that worked hard this week, or one that's been neglected? Take a moment to say something kind to it internally. That's Self-leadership in action."

Assign or refine the coming week's practice based on the check-in conversation. Always end with a specific, doable action.
