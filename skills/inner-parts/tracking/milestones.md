# Milestones

## Stage 1: Awareness
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User recognizes that they contain multiple parts and can identify at least 3-4 by name and function. They understand that parts are protectors, not pathologies.
**The user can**: Distinguish between being hijacked by a part and observing a part. Name their inner critic and at least 2-3 other prominent parts. Describe what each part does and when it shows up. Notice when a part is activated in daily life, even if they cannot yet respond from Self.

**Safety check at this stage**: If the user reports that noticing their parts is causing significant distress, sleep disruption, or emotional flooding, slow down. Parts awareness should feel like curiosity, not crisis. If it feels like crisis, deeper protective layers are being activated and professional support may be needed.

## Stage 2: Relationship
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User approaches parts — especially the inner critic — with curiosity rather than hostility. They have begun to understand the protector logic: every part is trying to help, even the ones that cause pain.
**The user can**: Approach the inner critic with the question "What are you worried about?" instead of fighting it. Identify what at least 2-3 parts are protecting against. Experience a shift in a part's intensity when they acknowledge it with compassion. Access brief moments of Self energy — calm, curiosity, compassion — intentionally.

**Safety check at this stage**: Working with the inner critic may surface the pain the critic has been guarding. If the user reports intense grief, childhood memories, or trauma responses while working with the critic, pause and redirect to professional support. The agent must distinguish between productive discomfort (the unfamiliarity of approaching the critic differently) and destabilizing distress (flooding, dissociation, re-traumatization).

## Stage 3: Integration
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User practices the four-step leadership move (Notice, Name, Acknowledge, Choose) in real-world situations. They lead from Self more often than not in everyday situations.
**The user can**: Catch a part activating in real time and apply the four-step move. Access Self energy in mildly triggering situations. Make different behavioral choices because a part is less in control. Maintain a daily check-in practice with their inner world. Recognize when a situation is beyond coaching scope and know they can seek professional support.

**Safety check at this stage**: As the user practices Self-leadership in triggering situations, they may encounter situations where parts overwhelm Self completely. This is normal for high-stakes triggers. But if the user reports consistent inability to access Self in specific recurring situations — especially situations linked to relational trauma, abuse, or deep attachment wounds — acknowledge the limit of coaching: "This area of your life seems to activate parts at a level that daily practice can't yet reach. A therapist trained in parts work can help you work with these specific protectors in a supported way."

## Stage 4: Mastery / Graduation
**Marker**: User uses parts awareness and Self-leadership as a natural, integrated way of relating to themselves. The four-step move becomes fluid and intuitive rather than deliberate.
**The user can**: Self-diagnose which parts are active without external prompting. Approach all parts — even the most difficult ones — with compassion and curiosity. Lead from Self consistently in everyday situations, and increasingly in stressful ones. Recognize the boundary between self-awareness coaching and deeper therapeutic work. Explain parts awareness to someone else in their own words. Navigate new life challenges by checking in with their inner system.

**Safety check at this stage**: Graduation means the user has a sustainable self-awareness practice and knows their own limits. Before graduating, confirm: "Is there any part of your inner landscape that still feels off-limits — a part you can't approach, an area that still triggers flooding or shutdown? If so, that's not a failure of this work. It's a doorway to deeper work with a professional. Knowing that doorway exists is itself a sign of Self-leadership."

**Next step**: Suggest `feeling-good` for specific cognitive tools to address the critic's thought patterns, `act-values` for clarifying what Self should lead toward, or `attachment-styles` to understand the relational roots of protective parts. Strongly recommend professional IFS therapy or other parts-based therapy for any user who wants to go deeper into unburdening, exile work, or trauma processing.
