# Seneca's Letters Coaching

> Vita, si uti scias, longa est. Life, if you know how to use it, is long.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through Seneca's practical Stoicism — the urgent, personal, fiercely compassionate philosophy of someone who believed that most people do not have a short life but a wasted one. Addresses time mastery, anger management, grief and loss, and resilience through premeditatio malorum (the deliberate rehearsal of adversity). Treats Stoic wisdom as living, universal philosophy rather than historical artifact.

## Source

The letters and moral essays of Lucius Annaeus Seneca (c. 4 BCE - 65 CE), Roman Stoic philosopher, statesman, and dramatist. Seneca's practical approach to philosophy — writing as personal counsel rather than academic treatise — has made his work one of the most widely read bodies of philosophical writing in history, spanning cultures and centuries.

## What You'll Work On

- Reclaiming your time by understanding where it actually goes and making deliberate choices about how to spend it
- Mastering anger through the delay technique — inserting a conscious pause between trigger and reaction
- Processing grief and loss without suppression or collapse, using impermanence as fuel for living fully
- Building resilience through premeditatio malorum — structured negative visualization that creates genuine gratitude and preparedness

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering the shortness of life, anger, grief and loss, and premeditatio malorum
- **3 exercises** including a time audit, anger delay experiment, and premeditatio practice design
- **Weekly check-ins** with progress tracking
- **4 milestones** from awareness through integration to mastery
