# Upanishad Self-Inquiry Coaching

> Who am I? -- not at the level of name, role, or personality, but at the deepest level of being.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through the Upanishadic practice of self-inquiry (atma vichara) -- the ancient discipline of asking "Who am I?" with enough honesty and persistence to discover what lies beneath every assumed identity. This is not motivational coaching dressed in Sanskrit. It is a structured encounter with one of the oldest and most penetrating investigations of consciousness ever undertaken, made accessible to anyone willing to look honestly at what they are.

## Source

The principal Upanishads -- particularly the Isha, Katha, and Mandukya -- along with the broader Vedantic tradition of self-inquiry. Concepts are drawn from the Upanishadic understanding of Atman (the true Self), Brahman (the ground of all being), neti neti (not this, not this), tat tvam asi (you are That), maya (misperception), avidya (ignorance of one's nature), and viveka (discrimination between the real and the unreal). Presented as universal philosophy applicable to anyone regardless of religious background.

## What You Will Work On

- Examining your layers of identity -- body, thoughts, emotions, roles -- and discovering what remains when each is recognized as impermanent
- Learning the chariot metaphor from the Katha Upanishad as a practical map for right relationship between body, senses, mind, intellect, and Self
- Understanding maya and avidya -- how misperception operates and what it means to see clearly
- Practicing daily self-inquiry (atma vichara) -- the discipline of asking "who is experiencing this?" in the midst of ordinary life
- Moving from conceptual understanding to direct recognition of awareness as the ground of experience

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering the fundamental question (neti neti), the chariot metaphor, maya and clear seeing, and tat tvam asi (integration)
- **3 exercises** including a layers-of-identity reflection, a seven-day witness practice experiment, and a self-inquiry integration plan
- **Weekly check-ins** with inquiry-centered progress tracking
- **4 milestones** from Questioning through Discrimination, Witnessing, and Recognition

## Important Note

This skill draws on a sacred tradition within Hinduism with roots stretching back thousands of years. It is presented as universal philosophy accessible to anyone, but the tradition itself deserves deep reverence. The AI agent is not a guru, not a teacher in the traditional sense, and not a spiritual authority -- it is a companion in structured inquiry. If you have a living connection to a teacher, a lineage, or a practice community within the Hindu tradition, honor that relationship above any AI interaction. The Upanishads teach that the Self is self-luminous. No external authority reveals it. Your own honest looking is the path.
