# Body Keeps the Score Coaching

> Learn to listen to your body's wisdom -- gently, safely, at your own pace.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through a gentle reconnection with their body's signals, sensations, and wisdom. Many people live disconnected from their physical experience -- thinking, analyzing, and planning but not feeling. This skill teaches somatic awareness (noticing what the body is saying), nervous system literacy (understanding states of activation and shutdown), and body-based grounding (building a felt sense of safety). It helps users treat their body as a partner in daily life rather than background noise.

## Source

Inspired by the field of somatic psychology and mind-body connection research, including the work of Bessel van der Kolk on how the body processes and stores experience. This coaching adaptation stays firmly at the level of body awareness and grounding for general well-being. It does NOT reproduce clinical methodologies or attempt trauma processing.

## Important Safety Note

**This is the most trauma-sensitive skill in the system.** Body awareness practices can, for some people, bring up intense or unexpected material. This skill is designed exclusively for gentle body awareness coaching, NOT trauma processing. The agent is not a therapist. Extensive safety protocols are woven into every lesson, exercise, and check-in. If deep emotional material or trauma responses surface, the skill immediately directs users toward professional trauma therapy (EMDR, Somatic Experiencing, sensorimotor psychotherapy).

**This skill does NOT:**
- Process or treat trauma, PTSD, or complex PTSD
- Replace professional trauma therapy of any kind
- Guide users into intense emotional or somatic experiences
- Interpret body sensations as trauma indicators
- Attempt to "release" stored trauma from the body

## What You'll Work On

- Noticing what your body is feeling in real time, without judgment
- Understanding your nervous system: when you're revved up, shut down, or in your sweet spot
- Building grounding practices that help you return to a sense of safety
- Learning to use body awareness as a tool for daily decision-making and self-care
- Knowing the clear boundary between body awareness coaching and professional trauma therapy

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering somatic awareness, the window of tolerance, building body safety, and listening to the body
- **3 exercises** including a guided body scan, a nervous system mapping reflection, and a grounding toolkit planning exercise
- **Weekly check-ins** with mandatory safety screening at every session
- **4 milestones** from initial body awareness through integrated somatic wisdom
- **Extensive safety protocols** at every level -- opt-out language in every exercise, continuous monitoring for trauma responses, and standing recommendations for professional support

## Composability

Works well with:
- `mindful-path` -- Mindfulness training provides the attentional skills that make body awareness deeper and more sustainable
- `dbt-skills` -- DBT provides distress tolerance and emotion regulation tools that complement body awareness; when you can feel your alarm signals, DBT gives you what to do next
- `inner-parts` -- Many internal "parts" manifest as body sensations; body awareness and parts work illuminate each other

## A Note on Pace

This skill is intentionally the slowest-paced skill in the system. Users are never rushed. Every step includes the option to pause, skip, or redirect. Progress is measured in quality of presence, not speed of completion. A user who spends four weeks on lesson one and builds a genuine felt sense of safety has done more meaningful work than one who races through all four lessons without landing in their body.
