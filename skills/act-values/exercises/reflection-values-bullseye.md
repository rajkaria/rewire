# Exercise: Values Bull's-Eye

## Type
reflection

## When to Use
After Lesson 3 (Values as Your Compass). Use when the user has identified their core values and needs to assess how aligned their current life actually is.

## Setup
Agent says: "You've got your values. Now let's see how you're actually living them. I'm going to walk you through a bull's-eye exercise. Imagine a dartboard for each area of your life. The center is 'fully living this value.' The outer ring is 'totally disconnected from it.' We're going to be honest about where the darts are landing right now."

## The Exercise

### Step 1: Name the domains
Agent says: "Let's look at four areas: work/education, relationships, health/self-care, and personal growth/leisure. For each one, tell me which of your core values is most relevant."

{Wait for user response}

### Step 2: Rate current alignment
Agent says: "Now, for each area, honestly rate yourself on a 1-10 scale. 10 means you're living that value fully in this domain. 1 means you're completely disconnected from it. Don't judge yourself — this is just a snapshot, not a verdict. Start with work."

{Walk through each domain, one at a time}

### Step 3: Identify the gap
Agent says: "Look at your scores. Where's the biggest gap between what matters and how you're living? That's not a failure — that's a compass pointing you toward where to focus. Which gap stands out to you?"

{Wait for user response}

### Step 4: Name the barrier
Agent says: "For that biggest gap — what's getting in the way? Is it a thought that hooks you? A feeling you avoid? A practical barrier? Name it as specifically as you can."

{Wait for user response}

## Branching Responses
- If the user struggles to rate themselves: "There's no right answer here. If you feel like you're living this value about half the time, that's a 5. If it barely shows up in your daily life, maybe it's a 2 or 3. Trust your gut."
- If the user rates everything low and seems discouraged: "I want to point something out. The fact that you can identify these gaps means you know what matters. That's not nothing — that's the foundation. A lot of people can't even name their values. You can. Now we just need to close the gap, one small step at a time."
- If the user rates everything high: "That's great if it's honest. Let me check — is there an area where you're telling yourself a story about alignment that might not hold up under scrutiny? Sometimes we confuse wanting to live a value with actually living it."

## Debrief Questions
1. "What surprised you about your scores? Any domain more misaligned than you expected?"
2. "If you could move one score up by just 2 points in the next month, which would have the biggest impact on your sense of meaning?"
3. "What's one thing you're doing right now — even a small thing — that already aligns with your values? Let's make sure we anchor on what's working, not just what's missing."

## Real-World Action
"Pick the domain with the biggest gap. Design one small action this week that would move your score up by even one point. Write it down with a specific day and time."

## Report Back
"When we talk next, I'll ask you: did you do the one thing? What did it feel like? And did your experience of that domain shift at all, even slightly?"
