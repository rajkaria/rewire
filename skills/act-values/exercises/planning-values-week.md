# Exercise: Values-Aligned Week

## Type
planning

## When to Use
After Lesson 4 (Committed Action). Use when the user has clarified values and is ready to translate them into daily behavior.

## Setup
Agent says: "You know what matters to you. Now let's design a week where your actions actually match your values. This isn't about overhauling your life — it's about threading small, intentional moves into the life you already have."

## The Exercise

### Step 1: Map values to life domains
Agent says: "Let's take your top 3 values. For each one, tell me: where in your weekly routine does this value have the most opportunity to show up? Think about work, relationships, health, and personal time."

{Wait for user response}

### Step 2: Design micro-actions
Agent says: "For each value, I want you to design one specific action you can take this week. Make it small enough that you'd rate your confidence at 8 out of 10 or higher. Not 'be more creative' — more like 'spend 20 minutes drawing on Thursday evening.' Give me your three actions."

{Wait for user response}

### Step 3: Pre-identify the barriers
Agent says: "Now the important part. For each action, what thought or feeling is most likely to show up and try to stop you? Name the barrier honestly. 'I'll be too tired.' 'My mind will say it's pointless.' 'I'll feel anxious and want to skip it.' What's yours for each?"

{Wait for user response}

### Step 4: Plan the response
Agent says: "For each barrier, decide right now how you'll respond. You've got defusion skills — you can notice the thought without obeying it. What will you say to yourself when that barrier shows up? Something like: 'There's the too-tired thought. Thanks, mind. I'm going to do this for just five minutes and see what happens.'"

{Wait for user response}

## Branching Responses
- If the user overcommits: "I love the ambition, but let's be honest about capacity. If you try to do too much and fail, your mind will use that as evidence that this doesn't work. Pick the three that matter most. You can always add more next week."
- If the user picks only easy actions: "These are good starts, but I want to check — is there one action here that actually makes you a little uncomfortable? Growth lives at the edge of your comfort zone. Can we find one action that has just a little bit of fear attached to it?"
- If the user has a breakthrough designing their week: "This is exactly it. You're not waiting to feel different — you're choosing to act different. That's the whole game. Now let's make sure these actually happen."

## Debrief Questions
1. "How many of your three actions did you complete this week?"
2. "For the ones you did — what showed up internally, and how did you handle it?"
3. "For any you missed — what pulled you off course? Was it the barrier you predicted, or something unexpected?"

## Real-World Action
"Execute the three values-aligned actions this week. When barriers show up, use your defusion skills. After each action, take 30 seconds to notice how it felt to move toward your values. Was there satisfaction? Relief? Pride? Let it land."

## Report Back
"Next session I'll ask: which actions happened, which didn't, and what did you learn about the gap between intention and action? We're not looking for perfection — we're looking for patterns we can work with."
