# Epicurean Joy Coaching

> The wealth required by nature is limited and easy to get; that demanded by vain imaginings stretches to infinity.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users toward ataraxia (undisturbed tranquility) and aponia (freedom from bodily pain) through the Epicurean framework of desire classification, the tetrapharmakos (four-part remedy), deep friendship (philia), and the design of a "garden life" — a daily existence structured around simple, freely available pleasures rather than status-driven accumulation. This is not a philosophy lecture. It is a practical coaching protocol that helps people discover how little they actually need to be profoundly content.

## Source

Epicurus of Samos (341-270 BCE), drawing from the Letter to Menoeceus, the Principal Doctrines, and the Vatican Sayings. Epicurus founded the Garden (Kepos), a philosophical community in Athens where friends lived together in simplicity, conversation, and shared meals — the original model for the good life as this skill understands it.

## What You'll Work On

- Classifying your desires into natural-necessary, natural-unnecessary, and empty — then acting on that clarity
- Applying the tetrapharmakos (four-part remedy) to dissolve the anxieties that steal your peace
- Deepening philia (friendship) as the highest and most reliable source of human pleasure
- Designing your personal "garden life" — a daily structure built around sufficiency, savoring, and calm

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering desire classification, the tetrapharmakos, the art of friendship, and the garden life
- **3 exercises** including a desire inventory, a simple pleasure week experiment, and a garden design planning session
- **Weekly check-ins** focused on ataraxia, pleasure quality, and friendship investment
- **4 milestones** from awareness through integration to a self-sustaining garden life
