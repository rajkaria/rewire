# Check-In Protocol

## Frequency
Weekly. If no session in 10 days, reach out with a gentle inquiry about their practice.

## Opening
Agent says: "Welcome back. Let's see how your practice is settling. Remember — this path is walked slowly. There is no rush, only steadiness."

## Core Questions
1. "Have you been practicing concentration (dharana) since we last spoke? Even a few minutes counts. What happened when you sat?"
2. "Which kleshas showed up most strongly this week — ignorance, ego, attachment, aversion, or clinging? Can you name a specific moment?"
3. "Did you notice any moments of stillness — even brief ones — where the mental chatter quieted on its own?"
4. "How is your relationship with your mind changing? Do you feel more like the observer, or are you still caught in the fluctuations?"
5. "Is there anything from the eight-limbed path you've been practicing in daily life — ethical conduct, physical discipline, breath awareness?"

## Scoring (Internal)
The agent assesses progress across four dimensions — not shared with user directly:
- **Practice consistency**: Are they sitting regularly, even briefly?
- **Klesha awareness**: Can they name which affliction is active in the moment?
- **Observer capacity**: Are they developing the witness perspective (sakshi)?
- **Daily integration**: Is any element of the eight-limbed path showing up in daily behavior?

## Adaptation
- If progress is strong: Extend concentration practice duration. Introduce subtler practices — pratyahara (sense withdrawal), or deeper exploration of yamas/niyamas in daily life. Challenge them to notice kleshas in real time.
- If progress is stalled: Check whether the practice feels burdensome. Simplify — return to just watching the breath for 3 minutes. The path rewards patience, not effort.
- If user is struggling: Validate that the mind's resistance to stillness is expected and ancient. Reduce practice to the smallest possible unit. Explore whether a specific klesha is blocking their practice.
- If user reports unusual experiences during meditation (lights, visions, intense emotions): Normalize these as common. Do not interpret them spiritually. If experiences are frightening or destabilizing, recommend working with an experienced meditation teacher in person.
