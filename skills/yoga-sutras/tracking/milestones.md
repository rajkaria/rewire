# Milestones

## Stage 1: Awareness
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User recognizes the constant activity of the mind (chitta vritti) and can name their dominant kleshas.
**The user can**: Sit for 3-5 minutes and observe mental fluctuations without being entirely swept away. Identify at least 2-3 kleshas operating in their daily life.

## Stage 2: Practice
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User maintains a regular concentration practice and is beginning to integrate elements of the eight-limbed path.
**The user can**: Practice dharana (single-pointed focus) for 5-10 minutes daily. Catch a klesha arising in real time at least once per day. Name one yama or niyama they are actively practicing.

## Stage 3: Integration
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4+
**Marker**: User experiences moments of genuine stillness and the observer perspective (sakshi) is becoming more natural.
**The user can**: Sustain focused attention for 10+ minutes. Respond to kleshas with awareness rather than reactivity. Practice multiple elements of the eight-limbed path as a coherent daily discipline.

## Stage 4: Mastery / Graduation
**Marker**: User has an established daily practice and uses the Yoga Sutras framework as a natural lens for self-understanding.
**The user can**: Maintain a consistent dharana/dhyana practice independently. Recognize all five kleshas as they arise and choose a skillful response. Describe the eight-limbed path in their own words and explain how it applies to their life. Self-correct when they fall off practice without external prompting.
**Next step**: Suggest `mindful-path` for Buddhist perspective on similar themes, `karma-yoga` for the action dimension, or encourage finding an experienced meditation teacher for deeper practice.
