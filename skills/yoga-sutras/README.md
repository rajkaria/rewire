# Yoga Sutras Coaching

> Yogash chitta vritti nirodhah. Yoga is the stilling of the fluctuations of the mind.

## What This Skill Does

Coaches users through the core teachings of Patanjali's Yoga Sutras — the nature of mind fluctuations (vrittis), the five mental afflictions (kleshas), the eight-limbed path (ashtanga yoga), and the practice of concentration leading to stillness (dharana and dhyana). This is not a summary of yoga philosophy or a guide to physical postures. It is a structured coaching protocol that guides an AI agent to walk someone through the systematic training of their own mind, with real-world exercises and weekly accountability.

## Source

The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, composed approximately 2,000 years ago, are among the most precise and systematic texts on the human mind ever written. In 196 concise aphorisms (sutras), Patanjali maps the nature of mental activity, diagnoses the root causes of suffering, and prescribes a comprehensive path of practice. The text draws on and synthesizes earlier traditions of yogic practice within the Hindu philosophical landscape. It remains a living text — studied, practiced, and transmitted in unbroken lineage to this day.

## What You'll Work On

- Observing the fluctuations of your own mind (chitta vritti) and learning to witness them rather than be swept away
- Identifying the five kleshas (afflictions) — avidya, asmita, raga, dvesha, abhinivesha — and recognizing how they operate in your daily experience
- Understanding and beginning to practice the eight-limbed path (ashtanga yoga) — from ethical foundations (yamas and niyamas) through concentration (dharana) and meditation (dhyana)
- Building the capacity for single-pointed attention through daily dharana practice
- Developing a personal, sustainable practice that integrates multiple dimensions of the path

## Structure

- **4 lessons** covering the restless mind, the five afflictions, the eight-limbed path, and concentration and stillness
- **3 exercises** including a klesha inventory, daily concentration practice, and eight-limbs integration planning
- **Weekly check-ins** with progress tracking
- **4 milestones** from initial observation through integrated daily practice

## A Note on Approach

This skill presents the Yoga Sutras as a living tradition of practical wisdom rooted in Hindu philosophy. The Sanskrit terminology is used alongside English to honor the precision of the original language and build genuine fluency in the tradition's vocabulary. The philosophy is presented as universally accessible — it does not require adherence to any religious framework — while maintaining deep respect for its sacred origins. The coaching tone mirrors the quality of the Sutras themselves: calm, precise, systematic, and always oriented toward practice. An AI agent is not a guru. This skill supports personal practice; it does not replace a teacher-student relationship within the tradition.
