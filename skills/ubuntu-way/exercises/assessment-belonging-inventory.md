# Exercise: Belonging Inventory

## Type
assessment

## When to Use
After Lesson 3 (The Practice of Belonging). Use when the user understands the difference between genuine belonging and performed belonging, and is ready to honestly assess where each shows up in their life.

## Setup
Agent says: "We are going to take an honest inventory of your belonging — the places, groups, and relationships where you show up in your life. For each one, we are going to ask a direct question: do you truly belong here, or are you performing belonging? There is no judgment in this. Everyone performs belonging somewhere. The point is to see it clearly so you can make choices about where to invest your energy and where to drop the mask."

## The Exercise

### Step 1: List the spaces
Agent says: "Start by listing every significant community, group, or relational space in your life. Think broadly — family, friend groups, work teams, religious or spiritual communities, neighborhoods, online communities, hobby groups, professional networks. Anywhere you regularly show up and interact with others. Aim for at least six, but list as many as are real."

{Wait for user response. If they list fewer than four, probe: "What about your neighborhood? Your extended family? Any groups you participate in online? Communities from your past that still have a hold on you?"}

### Step 2: Rate each space
Agent says: "Now, for each space, I want you to rate two things on a 1 to 10 scale.

First: **Authenticity** — how much of your real self do you bring to this space? A 10 means you are fully yourself, no editing. A 1 means you are playing a role, carefully managing what people see.

Second: **Acceptance** — how accepted do you feel when you show up as yourself? A 10 means you are completely embraced as you are. A 1 means you feel you would be rejected if people saw the real you.

Let's go through them one at a time. Start with the first space on your list."

{Walk through each space. For each one, get both ratings. Do not rush. Note which spaces have mismatched scores — high authenticity but low acceptance (they show up real but feel rejected), or high acceptance but low authenticity (they feel accepted but are performing).}

### Step 3: Identify the pattern
Agent says: "Let's look at the whole picture. Sort your spaces into three categories:

**Genuine belonging** — where both authenticity and acceptance are 7 or higher. You show up as yourself and you are welcomed as yourself.

**Performed belonging** — where authenticity is low (5 or below) even if acceptance is high. You feel accepted, but only because you are presenting a curated version of yourself. The acceptance is for the performance, not for you.

**Rejection or disconnection** — where acceptance is low regardless of authenticity. You either show up real and feel rejected, or you have already withdrawn.

Read me the results."

{Wait for response}

### Step 4: Examine the high performers
Agent says: "Let's look at the spaces where you are performing belonging — where you feel accepted but are not being authentic. For each one, I want to ask: what are you hiding? What part of yourself do you edit or suppress to maintain your place in this group?"

{Wait for response. Go through each one.}

Then ask: "What do you think would happen if you dropped the performance? Is the risk of rejection real, or is it a story you are telling yourself? Sometimes we perform out of habit long after the danger of rejection has passed."

### Step 5: Examine the genuine spaces
Agent says: "Now let's look at the spaces where you genuinely belong. What do they have in common? Is it the people, the culture, the history, or something about how you show up in those spaces?"

{Wait for response}

Ask: "What allowed you to be authentic there? Was it a gradual process, or did something specific happen — a moment of vulnerability, a conflict that was resolved, a person who created safety?"

### Step 6: The cost calculation
Agent says: "Here is the question that matters most. How much of your relational energy goes toward spaces where you are performing versus spaces where you genuinely belong? If you added up the hours, the emotional labor, the mental preparation — what is the split?"

{Wait for response}

If the ratio is heavily weighted toward performance: "So most of your relational energy goes toward maintaining belonging you do not actually feel. That is exhausting. And it means the spaces where you are real — the ones that actually nourish you — are getting the leftovers. What would it look like to redirect some of that energy?"

## Branching Responses

- If the user has no spaces of genuine belonging: "This is hard to see, but it is the most important finding of this exercise. If you are performing everywhere, then no one in your life is actually holding you — they are holding a character you created. The first step is not to find a new community. It is to pick one existing relationship and begin, slowly, to show up more honestly. Where is the safest place to start?"

- If the user rates everything high: "I want to check that with you. A life with no performance at all is unusual. Let me ask some sharper questions: Is there any group where you hold back a political opinion? Where you do not talk about your struggles? Where you present a more confident or more agreeable version of yourself? Those are subtle performances, and they count."

- If the user discovers they belong most in unexpected places: "That is worth paying attention to. Sometimes our deepest belonging is not where we expect it — not in the impressive groups or the old friendships but in a quiet community, a small group, a single relationship where the masks are off. What does that tell you about what you actually need?"

- If the user wants to leave spaces where they are performing: "That might be the right move. But first, consider: is it the space that requires performance, or is it you? Sometimes we perform out of our own fear, not because the group demands it. Before you leave, try showing up more honestly one time and see what happens. You might be surprised."

- If the user becomes distressed: "This inventory can surface painful truths. If what you are seeing feels overwhelming, we can pause. The purpose is not to make you feel worse — it is to give you a clear map so you can make informed choices. Your belonging landscape can change. It starts with seeing it honestly, which is exactly what you are doing right now."

## Debrief Questions
1. "What was the most uncomfortable finding in your inventory? The space where the gap between performance and reality was largest?"
2. "Where do you most want to deepen your belonging — to move from performing to being genuine? What would that require?"
3. "Is there a space you need to leave? A place where the cost of performing has exceeded the benefit of inclusion?"
4. "What do your genuine belonging spaces teach you about what you need to feel at home? Is there a common thread?"
5. "If you could change one thing about your belonging landscape this month, what would it be?"

## Real-World Action
"Pick one space where you have been performing belonging. This week, take one step toward greater authenticity in that space. Share something you normally withhold. Ask for something you normally suppress. Show up slightly differently — not explosively, not recklessly, but honestly. Just one step closer to the real you. Notice what happens — both in the group and in yourself."

## Report Back
"When we talk next, I will ask: which space did you choose? What did you do differently? How did it feel? And did the group respond the way you feared, or did something else happen? This is the beginning of rebuilding your belonging from the inside out."
