# Milestones

## Stage 1: Recognition
**Timeframe**: Sessions 1-2
**Marker**: User can articulate the dichotomy of control in their own words and has begun to see where they spend energy trying to control what is not up to them.
**The user can**: Distinguish between what is within their prohairesis (judgments, choices, effort) and what is not (outcomes, other people's actions, external circumstances). Identify at least two or three situations in their current life where they are investing energy in the wrong column. Recognize that their emotional reactions often stem from judgments they add to events, not from the events themselves.

## Stage 2: Observation
**Timeframe**: Sessions 2-3
**Marker**: User actively notices their judgments and impressions, at least in retrospect, and is beginning to catch them closer to real time.
**The user can**: Trace a strong emotion backward through the chain — from the emotion to the judgment to the bare event underneath. Name their most common automatic impressions by their specific content (not vaguely, but the exact internal sentences). Apply the dichotomy of control to new situations without being prompted. Begin to experience moments of equanimity in situations that previously triggered strong reactivity.

## Stage 3: Practice
**Timeframe**: Sessions 3-4
**Marker**: User has established a daily practice (even minimal) and is catching impressions in real time with increasing frequency.
**The user can**: Pause between an impression and their response — even briefly — in at least some situations. Maintain a morning premeditatio or evening review practice with reasonable consistency. Notice when they are confusing influence with control and correct without coaching. Carry the practice through genuine difficulty — not just easy or low-stakes moments.

## Stage 4: Integration / Graduation
**Marker**: User applies Stoic principles as a natural framework for navigating daily life, without needing session prompts to remember.
**The user can**: Sort any situation into "up to me" and "not up to me" as a reflex, not an exercise. Examine and revise the judgments behind their emotional reactions in real time. Catch strong impressions before assenting to them and choose their response from prohairesis. Maintain a sustainable daily practice — morning preparation, midday attention, or evening review — independently. Explain the core principles to someone else in their own words, grounded in personal experience rather than philosophical jargon. Hold difficulty without adding unnecessary suffering — not by suppressing emotion, but by seeing clearly.
**Next step**: Suggest `stoic-meditations` for a contemplative journaling practice, `seneca-letters` for practical wisdom on time, mortality, and emotional balance, or `act-values` for a modern psychological flexibility framework that deepens the acceptance and committed action skills.
